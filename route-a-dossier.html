The leadership trap

You have plenty of information. What you're missing is intel.

Information is everywhere and most of it is noise. When you're making a critical call, raw data doesn't help you. You need three things working at once.

Sharpest thinkers

Minds that cut through polite consensus, force clarity, and ask the exact question you've been walking around for months.

Frontline operators

Instincts from people who actually navigated the pivot, the crisis or the positioning call you're facing today.

Unfair intel

Deep, compounding context on your specific business: your numbers, your team, your market friction.

  • Consultants charge €5,000 a day to tell you what you already know.
  • Your team operates with the exact same blind spots you do.
  • Friends are too polite to tell you where your logic breaks.
  • Generic AI gives average advice that fits everyone and helps no one.

Growth Intel fuses the three into one council, on call whenever the question arrives.

In practice

Any topic. Any hour. Full context.

Whether you're navigating pricing at 9 AM or a founder conflict late at night, your council is informed and ready.

You

"I'm thinking about cutting our enterprise tier to focus 100% on self-service PLG."

Elder 1 · Strategy

"Before you do, look at the numbers from last week. Three enterprise clients fund 80% of your runway. That impulse might be about avoiding the sales conversation. Be honest about that first. What specifically is breaking in the sales process?"

You

"...Fair point. But the sales cycle is killing our team's momentum."

Elder 2 · Positioning

"Then the problem is who you're selling to. You're pitching middle managers who need permission. Find the champion who has budget. Who was the actual buyer on that Tuesday deal you closed?"

Scope

What Growth Intel is not

  • Task execution. The elders think with you. They don't do admin.
  • Legal or financial advice where liability applies.
  • A decision machine. The council sharpens your thinking. The call is always yours.
